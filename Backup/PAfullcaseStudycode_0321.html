Designing the Payment Arrangement feature for the My Sprint App

Payment Arrangement in the My Sprint App

A Payment Arrangement allows eligible customers extra time to pay their past due balance, while still enjoying uninterrupted service.

Current Scenario
  • Inconsistent design experience
    The feature is not native to the app and takes them to the app web view. This hampers performance and leads to inconsistency in design. Read more about native and hybrid apps here
    • Design Inconsistencies as we move from Native app to the App web view
  • Obscure Placement
    The placement of the feature is not immediately apparent. (Refer to the image above)
  • Wordy explanations
    The messaging in the flow is too wordy. Users skipped them and called customer care to ask the same questions.
    • Too much explanation offered
Problem statement

How can we make the overall experience of using the Payment arrangement feature more clear and intuitive for users?

User and business goals
  • Reduce the call/chat volume related to Payment Arrangements.
  • Increase customer understanding of Payment Arrangements & the option when available.
  • Increase on-time payments
  • Decrease service suspensions
  • Increase NPS score (overall customer satisfaction)

Challenges
  • Seamless Integration
    The feature needed to be introduced into the app seamlessly without breaking existing experiences.
  • Sensitive Flow
    Payment Arrangement is a stressful last-minute decision. Any interruptions lead users to call customer care. Thus the messaging related to ineligibility or extra charges becomes sensitive.
  • Numerous use cases
    My Sprint App has over 10 million users. Incorporating all associated use cases into the design is a mountainous task.
Understanding of users

Our understanding of the target users came primarily from finance and customer service teams, based on their observations over the years.

Scenarios in which users make a payment arrangement
  • They are waiting on their next paycheck.
  • An unexpected big charge has thrown their budget off track.
  • They have ideal dates in mind to pay their bill which does not align with their current billing cycle.

Competitive Analysis

To identify how other companies handle our 2 biggest pain points: the placement of the Payment Arrangement feature and messaging related to it, a competitive analysis was done.

This included looking at our direct competitors like T-Mobile, AT&T, and other utility companies like Dominion Energy or Cox communications.

Key Insight: Placement of the Payment Arrangement feature
  • Secondary feature
    T-Mobile places it as a secondary feature in their main payment flow. Here, there is a high probability for users to overlook the feature.
    • Placement of Payment Arrangement feature on T-mobile
  • Pay Now-Pay Later approach
    This approach makes it very clear to the users that if they can't pay their bill now, they have the option to pay later.
    It doesn't require users to have a prior understanding of what a Payment Arrangement is.
    • Dominion Energy has the option to Pay Now or Later
Key Insight: Payment Arrangement Messaging
  • Break in the experience
    Both T-mobile and AT&T redirect their users to the FAQs page to learn more about Payment Arrangement.
    • T-Mobile redirects customers to Payment Arrangement FAQs
  • Succinct messaging in our current approach
    Thus we stuck with our current approach of showing messaging at the beginning of the flow but decided to make it more succinct.

Evaluating the designs through Research

At this point, we wanted to check how well our design direction is addressing the initially identified problems. Thus we decided to conduct a user research study with the following objectives in mind:

  • Do we need to call out the Payment Arrangement feature on the main dashboard for past due users?
    • CTA for Payment Arrangement on Dashboard
  • Is the messaging for Payment Arrangement (PA) being expressed clearly enough? Are users still skipping the important parts?
  • Are users able to make a Payment Arrangement by themselves?
Research Methodology

The study was conducted in an unmoderated manner using UserZoom.

  • Click Test
    50% of the users were shown the dashboard without CTA for Payment Arrangement and the rest with CTA. This was done to identify how many users end up in the Payment Arrangement flow.
  • Think out loud study to identify any breaks in the flow.
    Task: Try to schedule a Payment Arrangement for your past-due bill.
Key Insights from the study
  • With an explicit CTA for Payment Arrangement shown on the billing page, users entered the Payment Arrangement flow much more (79% compared to 57%).
  • The overall flow was simple enough and all the participants were able to complete it.
  • Messaging: Most participants read only the headlines and top 2 bullets. They were still skipping important information provided in the latter half related to Autopay and Late Fees.
    • Users were still skipping important parts related to Autopay and Late Fees

    Among all channels that Sprint offers to create a Payment Arrangement, My Sprint app reached the top.
  • Positive Play Store reviews

    • Play Store reviews about Payment Arrangement
Retrospective
  • Edge cases affecting the main use case
    We spent a lot of time thinking about the edge cases which in turn adversely affected our main use case.
  • Get Data early on
    The stakeholders brought up some points at crucial times which we had no data to counter with. Thus getting quick and dirty data early on in the process is very helpful.
  • Understand your PMs
    Talk to the PMs and understand how willing they are to take risks with new approaches and pushing the entire team.
